GORILLAS


Scientific Name: Gorilla gorilla (Western Gorilla) / Gorilla beringei (Eastern Gorilla)

Family: Hominidae

Habitat: Tropical rainforests of Central Africa


Gorillas are the largest of the primates and are closely related to humans, sharing about 98% of our DNA. They live in family groups called troops, led by a dominant silverback male. These intelligent animals are known for their expressive faces, gentle nature, and ability to use tools and communicate using sign-like gestures and vocalizations.

Gorillas are primarily herbivores, feeding on fruits, shoots, and leaves. They build nests on the ground or in trees each night.

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered (depending on species)

Fun Fact: Gorillas show emotions like joy and sadness and even mourn their dead.
