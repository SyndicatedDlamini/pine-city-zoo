GIRAFFES


Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis

Family: Giraffidae

Habitat: Savannahs, open woodlands, and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa


Giraffes are the world’s tallest land animals, reaching heights up to 18 feet (5.5 meters). Their long necks, though they have the same number of vertebrae as humans, allow them to browse leaves high in acacia trees. Each giraffe has a unique pattern of spots, like a fingerprint.

They live in loose herds and are generally peaceful and social. Despite their height, giraffes have to awkwardly spread their legs to drink water from a pond.

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Fun Fact: A giraffe’s heart can weigh up to 25 pounds and must pump blood all the way up that long neck to the brain!
