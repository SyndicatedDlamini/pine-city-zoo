WARTHOGS


Scientific Name:Phacochoerus africanus

Family: Suidae

Habitat:Savannahs, open plains, and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa


Warthogs are wild members of the pig family, recognized by their tusks, facial warts (actually protective pads), and bristly manes. Despite their rugged appearance, warthogs are generally peaceful grazers and often seen kneeling while feeding due to their short necks.

They live in burrows, which they typically back into so their tusks face outwards, ready to defend against predators. Warthogs often share their burrows with others and can run impressively fast when startled.

Conservation Status: Least Concern

Fun Fact:Warthogs use mud wallows to cool down and protect their skin from parasites.
