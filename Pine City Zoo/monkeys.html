MONKEYS


Scientific Classification: Varies by species (examples: Macaca, Capuchinus, Cercopithecus)

Family: Cercopithecidae (Old World monkeys) / Cebidae (New World monkeys)

Habitat: Forests, jungles, mountains, and even urban areas across Africa, Asia, and the Americas


Monkeys are a diverse group of primates, ranging from tiny pygmy marmosets to larger baboons. They are intelligent, social animals with complex communication systems. Most monkeys live in groups and use grooming to strengthen social bonds.

Old World monkeys (Africa and Asia) generally have narrow noses and no prehensile tails, while New World monkeys (Central and South America) often have grasping tails that help them swing through the trees.

Conservation Status: Varies by species

Fun Fact: Some monkeys use tools like rocks to crack nuts or sticks to catch insects.
