KOALAS


Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus

Family: Phascolarctidae

Habitat: Eucalyptus forests of eastern and southern Australia


Koalas are iconic marsupials native to Australia. Despite being called "koala bears," they are not bears at all. They spend nearly all their time in eucalyptus trees, which provide both their food and shelter. Koalas have a special digestive system that detoxifies the otherwise poisonous eucalyptus leaves.

They sleep up to 20 hours a day due to their low-energy diet and are mostly solitary animals. Baby koalas, called joeys, stay in their mother's pouch for about six months.

Conservation Status: Vulnerable
