PANDAS


Scientific Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca

Family: Ursidae

Habitat: Mountain forests in central China


Giant pandas are beloved for their distinct black-and-white fur and gentle temperament. Though they are classified as carnivores, their diet is over 99% bamboo. Pandas have a special “thumb”—an extended wrist bone—that helps them grip bamboo stems.

They are solitary animals, coming together only to breed. Female pandas give birth to tiny, blind cubs that weigh just a few ounces at birth.

Conservation Status: Vulnerable (thanks to ongoing conservation efforts)

Fun Fact: A panda can eat up to 40 pounds (18 kg) of bamboo a day!
