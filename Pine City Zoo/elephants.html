ELEPHANTS

Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana (African Elephant) / Elephas maximus (Asian Elephant)

Family: Elephantidae

Habitat: Grasslands, forests, and savannahs across Africa and Asia


Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. African elephants, with their large ears and tusks, roam the savannahs and forests of sub-Saharan Africa. Asian elephants, slightly smaller with smaller ears and less prominent tusks, inhabit tropical forests and grasslands across South and Southeast Asia.

Highly intelligent and social, elephants live in tight-knit family groups led by a matriarch—the oldest and often wisest female. They communicate through low-frequency rumbles and have incredible memories. Their trunks are versatile tools used for breathing, feeding, drinking, and even greeting each other.


Conservation Status: African Elephant – Vulnerable; Asian Elephant – Endangered

Fun Fact: Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors—a rare trait shared with only a few other species.
